What is it?

This lecture is about deriving the quadratic formula in a more intuitive matter. For those of you who don't remember, the quadratic formula is the formula

\[ r, s = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b²-4ac}}{2a}.\]

Here \(r\) and \(s\) is the roots of the function \(f(x) = ax² + bx + c\). The roots \(r\) and \(s\) represents where on the \(x\) axis \(f(x) = 0.\) This is illustrated in the picture below:
